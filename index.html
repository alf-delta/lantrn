How training works
in practice

Training sessions follow a clear structure. Children know what to expect, how to move through each part, and why it matters. This creates confidence, not pressure.

01

Assessment first

We observe how the child moves, focuses, and responds to load before placing them into a group or format.

02

Structured sessions

Each session follows a consistent rhythm — warm-up, focused work, recovery — so attention and movement quality stay intact.

03

Progression over time

Load and complexity increase gradually. The goal is adaptation, not exhaustion.

Structure creates safety. Safety allows real growth.

Konstantine’s approach

Pilates-trained coaching with a biomechanics-first lens — built for long-term, healthy movement during growth.

Konstantine built this program around how the body adapts while children grow: patterns first, intensity second.

The studio practice is connected to the historic Manhattan Pilates tradition — the same address where Joseph and Clara Pilates established their New York studio in 1928, stated plainly and without myth-making.

We use Pilates discipline as a foundation: precision, control, and progression — so training stays structured, calm, and responsible.
